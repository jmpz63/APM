## üìã **Certification Strategy**

### **Decision Matrix: AS9100 vs ISO 13485**

| Factor | AS9100 (Aerospace) | ISO 13485 (Medical) |
|--------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **Market Size** | Larger ($12B aerospace machining) | Medium ($8B medical device components) |
| **Pricing Premium** | 2‚Äì4√ó commercial rates | 2‚Äì3√ó commercial rates |
| **Lead Times** | Longer (12‚Äì26 weeks typical) | Medium (8‚Äì16 weeks typical) |
| **Complexity** | Very High (DFMEA, PFMEA, FAIR, config mgmt) | High (DHR, DMR, process validation) |
| **Audit Rigor** | Very High (annual surveillance + customer audits) | High (annual surveillance + FDA inspections if applicable) |
| **Material Traceability** | Extreme (mill certs, heat lot tracking) | High (lot tracking to patient level) |
| **Best Fit If...** | You have aerospace contacts or nearby OEMs | You have medical device contacts; cleanroom optional |

**Recommendation**: Start with **AS9100** if you have aerospace industry connections or proximity to aerospace OEMs (Boeing, Lockheed, Northrop, etc.). Choose **ISO 13485** if you have medical device contacts or prefer slightly less complex audit requirements.

**Alternative Strategy**: Pursue **ISO 9001** first (6‚Äì12 months), then upgrade to AS9100 or ISO 13485 (adds 6 months). This allows you to serve commercial customers while building certification infrastructure.

## üîê **Regulatory & Compliance Roadmap**

### **AS9100 Certification Path**

#### **Prerequisites**
- ISO 9001:2015 foundation (can pursue simultaneously with AS9100)
- Documented quality management system (QMS)
- At least 3 months of operating history with QMS in place

#### **Timeline (12‚Äì18 Months)**
```
Month 1‚Äì3: Gap Analysis & Planning
- Hire consultant
- Conduct gap analysis vs AS9100 requirements
- Develop implementation plan

Month 4‚Äì9: QMS Development
- Write Quality Manual, procedures, work instructions
- Implement document control system
- Train staff on QMS requirements
- Establish CAPA (Corrective and Preventive Action) process

Month 10‚Äì12: Internal Audits
- Conduct internal audits (process audits, product audits)
- Address nonconformances
- Management review meetings

Month 13‚Äì15: Pre-Assessment (Optional)
- Registrar conducts pre-assessment audit
- Identify gaps before formal certification audit

Month 16‚Äì18: Certification Audit
- Stage 1: Document review (remote or on-site, 1 day)
- Stage 2: On-site audit (2‚Äì3 days)
- Corrective actions (if needed)
- Certification issued

Ongoing: Surveillance Audits
- Annual surveillance audits (1 day)
- Recertification every 3 years (full audit)
```

#### **Key Requirements**
- **Configuration Management**: Control of design changes, part revisions, customer approvals
- **Risk Management**: DFMEA (Design Failure Mode Effects Analysis), PFMEA (Process FMEA)
- **First Article Inspection (FAI)**: AS9102 forms for new parts; full dimensional inspection + material certs
- **Traceability**: Lot/serial tracking from raw material to finished part
- **Calibration**: All measurement equipment with traceable calibration (annual or per OEM schedule)
- **Supplier Management**: Approved vendor list; supplier audits or ISO 9001 certs required

### **ISO 13485 Certification Path**

#### **Prerequisites**
- ISO 9001 foundation (recommended but not required)
- Medical device risk management framework (ISO 14971)
- Device Master Record (DMR) for each product family

#### **Timeline (12‚Äì18 Months)**
```

- Create Device Master Records (DMRs)
- Implement Design Controls (if applicable)
- Develop process validation protocols (IQ/OQ/PQ)

Month 10‚Äì12: Process Validation
- Validate critical manufacturing processes
- Equipment qualification (Installation, Operational, Performance)
- Document Device History Records (DHRs)

Month 13‚Äì15: Pre-Assessment
- Internal audits focused on medical device requirements
- Registrar pre-assessment (optional)

Month 16‚Äì18: Certification Audit
- Stage 1: Document review
- Stage 2: On-site audit (2‚Äì3 days)
- Corrective actions
- Certification issued

Ongoing: Surveillance & FDA
- Annual surveillance audits
- FDA establishment registration (if selling in US)
- Post-market surveillance (complaint handling)
```

#### **Key Requirements**
- **Risk Management**: ISO 14971 risk analysis for each device/component
- **Design Controls**: Design inputs, outputs, verification, validation (21 CFR 820.30)
- **Process Validation**: IQ/OQ/PQ for critical processes (machining, cleaning, sterilization if applicable)
- **Traceability**: Device History Record (DHR) for each lot; links material, process, inspection data
- **Sterilization/Biocompatibility**: If applicable (implants); outsource to specialized labs
- **Complaint Handling**: MDR (Medical Device Reporting) for adverse events; 30-day FDA reporting

### **ITAR Registration (Defense)**

#### **Requirements**
- **US Persons Only**: Employees must be US citizens or permanent residents (Green Card)
- **Facility Security**: Access control, visitor logs, IT security (no foreign nationals)
- **ITAR Registration**: Submit DS-2032 form to DDTC; annual fee $2,250
- **Export Compliance Officer**: Designated person (can be owner in small shop)

#### **Timeline (3‚Äì6 Months)**
```
Month 1: Assess Applicability
- Determine if parts fall under USML (US Munitions List)
- Consult with ITAR attorney if uncertain

Month 2‚Äì3: Facility & IT Security
- Implement access control (badge system, visitor logs)
- IT security: firewalls, password policies, no foreign cloud storage
- Employee screening: verify citizenship/residency status

Month 4‚Äì5: Registration
- Complete DS-2032 form (ITAR registration)
- Submit to DDTC; pay $2,250 fee
- Await approval (4‚Äì8 weeks typical)

Month 6: Customer Onboarding
- Obtain ITAR-controlled technical data under NDA
- Begin quoting defense projects
```

#### **Ongoing Compliance**
- **Annual Re-Registration**: Renew ITAR registration each year ($2,250)
- **Training**: Annual ITAR training for all employees
- **Export Compliance**: All shipments require export license or exemption (e.g., ITAR 120.45)
- **Audits**: DDTC can audit at any time; maintain compliance records
